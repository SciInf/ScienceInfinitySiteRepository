Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What do we teach?

We prepare middle school students in advanced topics in science to prepare them for competitions. Here are the topics that we cover.

All level 1 subjects cover material from regular to honors level and level 2 subjects cover material from honors to AP level. Here are the subjects that we cover.

  1. Biology 1 and 2
  2. Physics 1 and 2
  3. Chemistry 1 and 2
  4. Earth Science
  5. Energy

Q: What does the schedule look like? What activities do we do?

For each subject, we hold a class every other sunday for 90 minutes.

  • A good portion of the class is spent in traditional classroom setting discussing topics on the subjects.
  • There is assigned homework and pop quizzes. Considerable self study outside classroom is expected.
  • About once a month, there is scheduled testing to assess learning of the topics covered.
  • There is oral testing using buzzers to gain practice for Science Bowl like competitions.

Q: Who can join?

Q: What Competitions will you prepare our students for?

We want to prepare the students for the following three competitions

  • NSF Science bee. http://northsouth.org. This is a written test. Some students excel in written tests. This is best for them. No space limitation. Everyone can participate.
  • Science Bowl. http://science.energy.gov/nsb. This is for students who are fast. The problems here are not deep, but fast paced buzzer style competition from broad areas of science. Three teams of five members each are only allowed to participate. The team members are all required to be from same school.
  • Science Olympiads - Schools send contingents of 15 to 20 members
  • YBTC - Individual Chemistry test and buzzer based with chapter, regional and national rounds

We believe these three competitions cover the interests of all motivated students.

Please note, the team formation is going to be based on performance, being a positive team member, providing positive contribution in the class, team composition etc. Please honor the coach’s decision and don’t take it personally. Coach’s decision is final, please don’t ask.
